Comparison of Demographic and Behavioral Factors


Obesity and Median Income:


There is a -0.55 correlation between State Population Obesity and State Median Household Income. This scatter chart allows us to easily see the downward trend in rates of Obesity as Household Income rises.

All of the following are questions for further study based on this preliminary analysis:
• Four of the top Five states for Obesity are in the deep south. Are there cultural factors influencing nutritional behaviors?
• If cost and availability factors are insignificant, is there a nutritional education component?
• How does cost and availability of food influence this relationship?


Notes:
Obesity data comes from the CDC’s Behavior Risk Factor System data for 2014.
Median Household Income data comes from the Census Bureau’s 2014 American Community Survey 1-year Estimate dataset.
